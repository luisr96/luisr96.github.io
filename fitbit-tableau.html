Fitbit Data Visualization

This is a simple project that I used to get familiar with Tableau. I went on walks with my dog between June 2020 and May 2021 and tracked some metrics like distance, duration, heart-rate, elevation gain and others. Going full screen at the bottom right makes the dashboard display better.

I decided to exclude Hour 22 because the only data point there was a 635 minute walk where I forgot to turn the fitbit off and it was skewing the data. Hour 0 doesn't show since there's no datapoints at midnight.

Some insights from the data:

  • There were some months where I lost the fitbit or hadn't used it in a long time which reflects in the per month graph.
  • Most of my walks were in the range of 20-40 min long.
  • I walked the most on Sundays and the least on Tuesdays.
  • I did get into Peak and Fat Burn heart rate zones but I was in the out-of-zone heart rate zone for most of the time.
  • The trendlines show that the earlier the walk, the more willing I was to climb up hills.
  • I also burned less calories earlier than later, but that correlation is less strong.
  • My heart-rate stayed around the same no matter what time I went on a walk.
This project was my practical intro to Tableau where I could start applying what I've been learning.
