Senior Design Project

A summary of my Spring 2021 capstone project

High-level overview

For our final project, our engineering team of UCF students worked with Steelcase, a large furniture manufacturer, to improve their international shipping processes. We coded a model, made recommendations based on data, wrote system requirements documentation, and continuously communicated with people in various disciplines to get a final product that we and our customers were happy with.

Software Solution

We deployed a packing algorithm program to model—and create instructions for—fitting furniture cargo into shipping containers as compactly as possible, minimizing shipping costs. The model simulates loading cargo on the floor (current methodology) but can also allows for stacking cargo on top of each other depending on certain dimensional and weight constraints. The stacking part of the project helped justify an ongoing ME project to reach even more packing efficiency.

Project Management

I was the software engineering lead for our student team. Our mentor recommended that we take the Agile approach, so we had frequent standup meetings and I had the responsibility of leading a lot of them. We used different organizational tools like Trello, Gantt charts and Discord to stay focused and work collaboratively. There was a lot of communication and support and I'm honored to say I had a great team.

Interdisciplinary Teamwork

Our student team consisted of both industrial and mechanical engineers with all sorts of different backgrounds and so we had to learn things about the other discipline. Besides our student team, we also met frequently with our mentors, packaging engineers, safety engineers, customers, etc. All of those gave me a better idea of what it means to work on a system as a part of a whole. We all learned from and taught each other, which was a really valuable experience.
